🕯️ Spell of the Week

🌙 This Week's Working
New Beginnings Candle Spell — Clearing the Path Forward
Best performed: On a new moon, or any Wednesday morning before noon.

You will need:
A white or light blue candle  ·  A piece of paper and pen  ·  A fireproof dish  ·  Rosemary or sage (fresh or dried)  ·  A pinch of sea salt

The working:
Begin by grounding yourself — take three slow, deep breaths and feel your feet on the floor. Light your candle and hold the paper in both hands. Write one thing you are ready to release — a fear, a habit, a relationship pattern, a belief about yourself that no longer serves you. Be honest. Be specific.

Sprinkle a pinch of sea salt over the words, then fold the paper away from you (fold it outward, symbolizing release). Hold it briefly in the candle flame — carefully — and place it in your fireproof dish to burn fully. As it burns, say aloud or in your mind:

"What was, releases. What comes, arrives with grace. I am clear. I am ready. So it is."

Let the candle burn for at least 20 minutes. When you're done, scatter the ashes outside or flush them — returning them to the earth or water completes the release.

📖 Introduction to Spellwork

Spellwork is the intentional direction of energy toward a desired outcome. It is not about bending reality to your will through force — it is about aligning yourself so completely with what you want to create that you become a clear channel for it to manifest. Every spell has the same core components: intention, energy, and release. Everything else — candles, herbs, words, timing — are tools that help you focus and amplify those three things.

🎯

Intention

The most powerful ingredient in any spell is the clarity and sincerity of your intention. Before you light a single candle, you must be absolutely clear about what you are calling in — and why. Vague intentions produce vague results. "I want more money" is less powerful than "I am open to and worthy of financial abundance flowing to me through joyful, aligned work." Write your intention out before every working. Read it aloud. Feel it in your body. That feeling of resonance IS the magic.

⚡

Energy & Raising Power

A spell without raised energy is just words and props. Energy must be raised — built up within you or the working space — and then directed toward your intention before release. Methods for raising energy include: breathwork, chanting, drumming, dancing, meditation, visualization, and emotional intensity. The stronger and more focused the energy you raise, the more potent the working. Many practitioners feel a physical sensation — heat, tingling, pressure in the hands — when energy is properly raised.

🕯️

Candle Magic

Candles are among the most accessible and versatile magical tools. The flame represents transformation — the alchemical process of converting one state into another. Color matters: white is all-purpose and purifying; red for passion and courage; green for growth and abundance; blue for calm, healing, and truth; black for banishing and protection; purple for spiritual work and psychic opening; pink for love and self-compassion; yellow for clarity and communication. Dress your candles with oils and herbs aligned to your intention to amplify their power.

🌙

Timing & Moon Phases

Timing your workings with natural cycles amplifies their power by aligning your personal energy with the larger rhythms of the universe. The moon's cycle is the most commonly used: the new moon is for new beginnings, setting intentions, and starting fresh. The waxing moon (growing) is for drawing things toward you — abundance, love, opportunity. The full moon is peak power — use it for manifestation, charging tools, and divination. The waning moon (shrinking) is for release, banishing, and letting go. The dark moon is for shadow work, deep rest, and inner reflection.

🌿

Herbs & Plants

Every plant carries its own energetic signature, shaped by its chemistry, its history of use, and its connection to planetary and elemental forces. Rosemary is protective and clarifying. Lavender soothes and opens psychic channels. Mugwort enhances dreams and divination. Basil draws abundance and love. Bay laurel amplifies wishes and victory. Sage clears and purifies. You don't need a full apothecary to begin — a few versatile herbs will serve most purposes. Always research any herb before burning or ingesting, especially if you are pregnant or have health conditions.

🛡️

Protection & Grounding

Before any magical working, grounding and protection are not optional — they are foundational. Grounding means connecting your energy to the earth, stabilizing yourself so you are working from a centered, rooted place rather than a scattered or depleted one. Visualize roots extending from your feet into the earth. Protection means establishing a clear energetic boundary around your working space and yourself. This can be as simple as calling in white light, casting a circle, burning protective herbs like rosemary or frankincense, or stating clearly: "Only that which is aligned with my highest good may enter this space."

🔓

Release & Trust

The final and most spiritually advanced step of any working is release — surrendering attachment to the outcome. This is where most beginners struggle. After raising your energy, stating your intention, and directing your focus, you must let it go completely. Clinging, obsessing, and constantly checking for results actually blocks manifestation by feeding energy into doubt rather than trust. "Set it and forget it" is not passive — it's an active choice to trust that what you've planted is growing, even when you can't see it yet. The universe works in its own timing.

⚖️

Ethics & Responsibility

With magic, as with all power, comes responsibility. The most important ethical principle in most traditions is simple: do not use magic to override another person's free will. Love spells designed to force a specific person to love you, workings designed to harm or manipulate — these create energetic debt and almost always backfire. Instead, focus your workings on yourself: your own clarity, your own healing, your own opening to receive what is meant for you. There is more than enough magic available when you work in alignment with the highest good of all.

💡 The most important truth about spellwork: You are the magic. Every tool, herb, candle, and phase of the moon is an amplifier — but the source of power is always you. Your belief, your focus, your emotional energy, and your alignment are what determine results. Start simple. Stay consistent. Document everything in your Book of Shadows.

📓 Your Book of Shadows

A Book of Shadows (BoS) is a witch's personal magical journal — a living record of your practice, your workings, your discoveries, and your growth. It is not a fixed rulebook passed down from above; it is something you build yourself, over time, through experience. No two Books of Shadows are alike. Yours reflects your path, your voice, and your magic. Below is a complete guide to starting yours from scratch.

📖 What Is a Book of Shadows?

Foundation · History · Purpose

The term "Book of Shadows" was popularized by Gerald Gardner, the founder of Wicca, in the mid-20th century — but the practice of keeping a magical journal is far older, found across virtually every magical tradition worldwide under different names: the grimoire in European ceremonial magic, the tao-te in Chinese alchemy, the ijala among Yoruba practitioners.

At its core, a BoS is a place where you record what you do, what you believe, what works, and what doesn't. It grows with you. A page you wrote as a beginner will look very different from one written five years into your practice — and both have value. The only requirement is that it is honest and it is yours.

🛠️ Choosing Your Book

Physical · Digital · What to Look For

Your BoS can be anything that feels sacred to you. Many practitioners use a beautiful hardcover journal or a three-ring binder (practical for adding pages and sections). Others keep a digital BoS in Notion, Obsidian, or even a private blog. Some keep both — a digital record for ease and a handwritten journal for ritual use.

Whatever you choose, make it feel special. Decorate the cover. Write your name on the inside. Consider dedicating it in a simple ritual — light a candle, hold the book, and state your intention for your practice. This act of consecration signals to your subconscious (and to the universe) that this is sacred space.

📋 What to Include — A Starter Structure

Sections · Organization · What Goes Where

There is no single right way to organize a BoS. Below is a suggested structure for beginners — adapt it freely as your practice develops.

  • Dedication page — Your intention for your practice. Who you are as a practitioner, what you believe, what you seek.
  • Beliefs & principles — Your personal ethical framework. What you will and won't do. The traditions or paths that resonate with you.
  • The wheel of the year — The eight sabbats (Samhain, Yule, Imbolc, Ostara, Beltane, Litha, Lughnasadh, Mabon) with dates, meanings, and how you choose to honor them.
  • Moon phases — Your guide to working with lunar cycles. Notes from actual moon rituals you've performed.
  • Correspondences — Colors, herbs, crystals, planets, days of the week, and their magical associations. This becomes your personal reference library.
  • Spells & rituals — Every working you perform: date, moon phase, intention, ingredients, method, and results. This is the most valuable section over time.
  • Dreams & divination — Tarot readings, dreams, synchronicities, visions, and intuitive hits. Patterns you notice over time.
  • Herb & crystal guides — Your growing personal reference for plant and stone magic, built from your own research and experience.
  • Prayers, chants & invocations — Words that carry power for you. Original or found, attributed honestly.
  • Reflections & lessons — What you're learning. What surprised you. What didn't work. Your evolution as a practitioner.

🌙 Recording Your Workings

The Most Important Practice · What to Write · When to Write

The habit of documenting every working is what separates practitioners who grow quickly from those who stay stuck. Write in your BoS immediately after any ritual or spell while the details are fresh. Over time, you will begin to see patterns — which moon phases produce the strongest results for you, which herbs resonate most with your energy, which types of workings you are naturally gifted at.

For each working, record at minimum: the date, the moon phase, your emotional state going in, the intention, what you did, and how you felt afterward. Then return to that entry days or weeks later and add a note about what manifested. This feedback loop is the foundation of genuine magical development.

✨ Making It Sacred

Personalization · Ritual · Energy

Your BoS is a magical object in its own right. Treat it accordingly. Keep it in a dedicated space — wrapped in cloth, kept on your altar, or stored somewhere private and intentional. Many practitioners cleanse their BoS periodically with smoke, moonlight, or by placing a cleansing crystal on top of it overnight.

Decorate its pages. Press herbs between the leaves. Paste in images that represent your path. Write in your own hand whenever possible — the physical act of handwriting is itself a form of energy direction. Your BoS should feel, when you hold it, like a living piece of your magical self. Because it is.

💡 Jennifer's advice: Don't wait until you feel ready or until you have the perfect journal. Start today, with whatever you have. The Book of Shadows you actually write in is infinitely more powerful than the perfect one you're still waiting to begin. Every entry is a thread in the tapestry of your practice. Start weaving.
